## Factors to consider when hiring Next.js developers and agencies

Here are some key factors to consider to ensure you find the right fit for your project:

### Reputation

A developer's or agency's reputation can be a good indicator of their expertise and quality of work. Check for testimonials from past clients and online reviews to get a better understanding of their track record.

### Budget

Next.js developers and agencies can vary significantly in terms of pricing. It's crucial to have a clear budget in mind and find a professional or agency that aligns with your financial constraints.

### Tech stack and proficiency with Next.js

Evaluate the developer's or agency's proficiency with Next.js and their familiarity with the technologies and tools you plan to use in your project. This will ensure a smooth development process and seamless integration.

### Domain expertise

If your project requires specific domain knowledge, such as building a headless website or a complex application like a ride-hailing platform, it's best to work with experts who have experience in that domain. Check their portfolio and years of experience to gauge their suitability.

### Nature of the project

Simple projects like a portfolio or landing page may be well-suited for a skilled freelancer, while complex projects like the MVP for a startup may require the resources and expertise of an agency. Consider the project details, such as the number of developers required, when making your decision.

## FAQs

### What is the best place to hire freelancers?

Popular platforms for hiring freelancers include Upwork, Freelancer.com, Fiverr, and Toptal. You can also access expert Next.js freelancers through [Prismic’s partner program](https://prismic.io/hire-a-developer).

### Who hires freelancers the most?

Freelancers are hired by various clients, including startups, small businesses, agencies, and large enterprises. There s a high demand for freelancers because of the specialized skills, cost-effectiveness, and flexibility they provide.

### Should I hire a freelancer or an agency?

The decision to hire a freelancer or an agency depends on the nature and complexity of your project. A skilled freelancer may be sufficient for simple projects with well-defined requirements. However, an agency with a team of developers may be a better fit for larger projects that need ongoing support and maintenance.

### What questions should I ask before hiring a freelancer or an agency?

Questions to ask when hiring a Next.js freelancer or agency include:

- How much experience do you have with Next.js and other technologies required for the project?

- How do you communicate milestones with clients? Learn more about [how developers and agencies communicate with clients](https://prismic.io/blog/client-communication-guide).

- What is your project management approach? [Explore this article](https://prismic.io/blog/website-development-project-management) to learn how developers manage their website projects for quality and timely delivery.

- How do you handle changes or issues that occur during the project?

- What form of support do you offer during and after project delivery?

Additionally, request examples of their previous work and client references to gauge their expertise and quality of work.

PS: developers also have questions they’ll ask you. [Here are 10 of them](https://prismic.io/blog/discovery-questions-freelance-web-development), so you’ll know what to expect.

### What are the benefits of hiring a Next.js freelancer or an agency?

The benefits of hiring a Next.js freelancer or an agency include:

- Access to professionals with deep expertise and practical experience with Next.js that can implement best practices and deliver high-quality solutions

- Working with Next.js experts helps you avoid long and steep learning curves. This means you can launch projects faster, meet deadlines, and stay competitive

- Enables you to quickly build prototypes and validate ideas before full-scale development. This is especially useful for startups that need rapid prototyping and iteration for their MVPs
