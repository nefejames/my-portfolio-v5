## Why build a blog with Next.js

Let’s explore some reasons why Next.js is a tool worth using to build your blog.

- **Speed**: Next.js offers [fast page load times](https://prismic.io/blog/api-response-times) through its automatic code splitting, [server-side rendering](https://prismic.io/blog/what-is-ssr), and [static-site generation](https://prismic.io/blog/nextjs-ssr-vs-ssg) capabilities.
- **SEO**: Next.js's support for server-side rendering ensures that search engines can easily crawl and index your content, boosting your search rankings. _[Learn how to boost your website SEO with Prismic](https://prismic.io/blog/prismic-website-seo)._
- **Developer friendly**: Next.js provides a smooth development experience with features like hot module replacement, built in [font module](https://prismic.io/blog/understanding-how-to-minimize-web-fonts-negative-impacts), lazy loading, and more.
- **Built-in image optimization**: The custom [Next.js image component](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/api-reference/components/image) automatically optimizes images for different devices and screen sizes. This improves load times and the user experience, and ensures that your blog performs well across all devices.
- **Compatible with headless CMS platforms**: Next.js seamlessly integrates with popular [headless CMS platforms](https://prismic.io/blog/headless-cms) like Prismic, enabling you to create a fast, dynamic blog with [easy content management](https://prismic.io/blog/visual-editing-headless-cms).

## What features should my Next.js blog have?

Let’s explore six must-have features that can elevate your Next.js blog from good to great and improve your readers’ experience.

### Feature: Table of contents

![GIF of Table of Contents ](/portfolio/prismic/031-nextjs-blog-templates/image-1.gif)

Imagine diving into a long article, and you're looking for a specific section. It would be frustrating if you had to scroll down till the end because that was the only way to navigate the blog. Making it easy for readers to look for information is key, and this is where a [Table of Contents (TOC)](https://prismic.io/blog/nextjs-table-of-contents-tutorial) comes in.

A TOC improves [user experience](https://prismic.io/blog/ux-tips-for-developers) by allowing readers to quickly jump to different sections of your content. This feature is especially beneficial for long-form articles, tutorials, and guides.

Seeing as you’re reading this article, you’re likely seeing the TOC on the Prismic blog on the left side of this page (or at the top of the page if you’re on a mobile device). Thanks to that, you can easily navigate to the sections of the article that interest you the most.

### Feature: Rich media

Words are great, but sometimes a picture—or a video—really is worth a thousand words. Rich media can make your content more engaging and easier to understand.

You’ve read this article up to this point and I can safely assume that you’ve enjoyed reading it so far. I suspect that the images and gifs that I added have played a part in grabbing your attention and in keeping you engaged. Now, imagine if there were no images and gifs. You _may_ have kept on reading, but it may have not been as delightful an experience.

The point? Add [images, gifs, and videos](https://prismic.io/blog/next-gen-image-formats). However, don’t go overboard and include media files after every sentence. That would be overwhelming, leading to a bad user experience.

### Feature: Author profiles

![An image of blog author profile page](/portfolio/prismic/031-nextjs-blog-templates/image-4.webp)

You wouldn’t take medical advice from a random person on the street, right? You’d trust a certified doctor because of their credentials and expertise. The same principle applies to your blog. Adding author profiles establishes trust with your readers.

An author profile lets readers know who's behind the content. It also adds a personal touch to your blog posts, helps build trust with your audience, and is crucial for establishing authority in your niche.

Having an author profile also aids your blog's SEO through the principles of [Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness (E-E-A-T)](https://www.semrush.com/blog/eat-and-ymyl-new-google-search-guidelines-acronyms-of-quality-content/). It tells search engines that the content was created by an expert and helps search bots to accurately indexing your content, potentially boosting your search engine rankings.

## Tips and best practices for optimizing performance and SEO on a Next.js blog site

Performance and SEO are key for blogs. Here are some strategies you can use to improve these aspects of your blog.

### Optimize fonts, images, and third-party scripts

- Optimizing fonts, images, and third-party scripts is crucial for enhancing your site's performance. Use `next/font` ( [docs](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/building-your-application/optimizing/fonts)) to automatically handle font optimization and avoid issues like layout shift.
- Optimize images with `next/image` ( [docs](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/building-your-application/optimizing/images)), which ensures the right image size is delivered to the user's device, reducing load times.
- Load third-party scripts asynchronously using `next/script` ( [docs](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/building-your-application/optimizing/scripts)) to prevent them from blocking critical content.

### Optimize meta tags

Meta tags provide search engines with essential information about your pages, such as the title, description, and keywords. Use the `next/head` ( [docs](https://nextjs.org/docs/pages/api-reference/components/head)) component to add well-optimized meta tags to your blog.

### Add schema markup

Adding structured data like schema markup to your blog helps search engines understand your content better. This enhances search engines' comprehension of your content and improves your site's visibility in search engine results pages (SERPs).

### Add a `sitemap.xml` file

A sitemap helps search engines understand the structure of your website. Programmatically generate a `sitemap.js` or `sitemap.ts` file for your blog and submit it to Google Search Console to ensure efficient crawling and indexing.

### Add a `robots.txt` file

The `robots.txt` file tells search engine crawlers which URLs they can access on your site and which they should avoid. This helps manage the crawling of your site to ensure sensitive or non-essential pages aren't indexed.

### Monitor performance

Regularly use tools like [Google PageSpeed Insights or Lighthouse](https://prismic.io/blog/google-pagespeed-insights-vs-lighthouse) to monitor your blog’s performance, focusing on metrics like loading times, [Core Web Vitals](https://prismic.io/blog/how-to-improve-core-web-vitals), and overall performance scores.
