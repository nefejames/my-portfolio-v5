### 1\. Prismic

**Best for: AI-visible landing pages and developer-controlled content architecture**

![Image of Prismic homepage.](/portfolio/prismic/061-best-headless-cms-platforms/image-1.webp)

[Prismic](https://prismic.io/) is a headless CMS designed for launching fast, branded websites and landing pages in no time. It combines a flexible content platform with a dynamic page builder, allowing developers to create reusable components while marketers launch new content or update existing content independently.

#### Standout features

- [**Visual page builder**](https://prismic.io/page-builder): Lets you create and edit pages in real time, so you can see exactly how your content will look as you build it. You can assemble pages visually, like you would arrange slides in a deck.
- [**Slice Machine**](https://prismic.io/slice-machine): Prismic’s local development tool that lets you build and manage slices directly inside your project. Instead of configuring everything in the cloud first, you create reusable page sections alongside your code, test them locally, and then sync them to Prismic when ready.
- [**ABM landing page builder**](https://prismic.io/abm-landing-page-builder): Lets you generate hundreds of personalized landing pages in minutes. You build a reusable page blueprint with locked-in brand design and messaging, upload a CSV with your account data, and Prismic's AI generates personalized page variations for each account.
- [**SEO/GEO landing page builder**](https://prismic.io/seo-landing-page-builder): Converts a keyword list into hundreds of optimized, brand-consistent landing pages quickly, each tailored to a specific search intent or long-tail query.
- **[Migration API](https://prismic.io/features/migration):** For transferring content to Prismic in bulk, so you don’t start from scratch. You can migrate thousands of pages easily and import content across multiple locales.

#### Benefits for Marketers

- Visual page builder for real-time editing.
- ABM and SEO/GEO landing page builders for campaign scale.
- Built-in localization.

#### Benefits for Developers

- Slice Machine for code-first component development.
- REST API.
- Migration API for bulk content transfers.

#### Limitations

- Prismic is optimized for web and landing page use cases, so teams delivering content across multiple channels may need additional custom development and careful content structuring to make it work.
- It doesn’t include built-in integrations for things like e‑commerce or forms, so custom development is required.

#### Pricing

### 2\. Sanity

**Best for: flexible content operations & real-time collaboration**

![Image of Sanity homepage](/portfolio/prismic/061-best-headless-cms-platforms/image-2.webp)

[Sanity](https://www.sanity.io/) positions itself as a "Content Operating System" rather than a traditional headless CMS. This means that it provides a fully customizable backend for building, managing, and delivering content-driven applications, with tools for real-time collaboration and AI-powered workflow automation.

#### Standout features

- **[Sanity Studio](https://www.sanity.io/studio):** A content workspace that lets you control how your team creates and publishes content. It offers real-time collaboration and visual editing while keeping your content structured and your media organized.
- [**Content Agent**](https://www.sanity.io/content-agent): An AI agent that handles content operations at scale. It lets you audit, update, and transform hundreds or thousands of documents in minutes, while staying aware of your content structure and schema.
- [**Content Lake**](https://www.sanity.io/content-lake): A real-time database built for storing any JSON content. You can query, mutate, and manage the content you store in it using GROQ—Sanity’s open-source query language for JSON.

#### Benefits for Marketers

- Real-time collaboration with content teams.
- AI Content Agent for bulk content updates.

#### Benefits for Developers

- Fully customizable Studio built with TypeScript and React.
- GROQ query language.
- Schema-as-code with Git versioning.

#### Limitations

- Sanity can be complex to learn because its workflows and configurations take time to get used to.
- GROQ is not as familiar as GraphQL, so it requires some learning.

#### Pricing

- Free
- Growth ($15/month/per seat)
- Enterprise (custom)

### 6\. Directus

**Best for: database-first flexibility**

![Image of Directus homepage](/portfolio/prismic/061-best-headless-cms-platforms/image-6.webp)

[Directus](https://directus.io/) is an open-source headless CMS that wraps directly around your existing SQL database, turning it into a collaborative backend with instant APIs and a visual workspace for your entire team.

#### Standout features

- **[Visual data modeler](https://directus.is/backend/data-modeling):** Lets you define collections, fields, and relationships through an interface without touching your database directly. Your schema stays in your database; Directus just gives you a visual layer on top of it.
- **[SQL database support](https://directus.is/backend/dbs):** Instead of forcing you to use its proprietary data layer, you can bring your own SQL database, like MySQL, PostgreSQL, MS SQL, or SQLite. You retain full control over your schema, can use raw SQL queries when needed, and can plug Directus into both new projects and existing databases without restructuring your data.
- **[Automation builder](https://directus.io/toolkit/automate):** Lets you build workflows that trigger on data changes, schedules, or manual input, and connect external services without writing code.

#### Benefits for Marketers

- Visual workspace for managing live data without code.
- Automation builder for workflow triggers.

#### Benefits for Developers

- Bring-your-own SQL database.
- Instant REST and GraphQL APIs.
- Fully extensible via custom endpoints, hooks, and modules.

#### Limitations

- Since Directus doesn't come with a managed content layer out of the box, you have to connect your own SQL database first, and setting up custom database connections adds some technical overhead.

## How to Choose the Best Headless CMS in 2026: factors to consider

These are only some of the best overall headless CMSs out there, and while this is a short list you can work with, you still have to pick one in the end.

Let’s review the core factors that matter when choosing a platform.

### Considerations for developers

- **API type**: Check whether the CMS uses REST, GraphQL, or both. Your team’s familiarity and the complexity of your content model can make one API type a better fit than another.
- **Customizability and extensibility**: Evaluate how much you can extend the CMS with plugins or custom code
- **Development workflow**: How well does the CMS integrate with your local development environment and CI/CD pipelines?
- **Performance and scalability**: Consider API response times, CDN support, caching, and how the CMS handles large volumes of content.

### Considerations for marketers

- **Content workflow**: The CMS should make it easy for non-technical team members to create, edit, and publish content without constant developer support.
- **Visual editing and personalization tools**: Can the platform let you manage content visually, while also delivering personalized experiences for different audiences?
- **Localization and multi-channel support**: Can the CMS handle multiple languages and deliver content consistently across all platforms and regions?
- **Integrations:** Check whether the CMS connects with the tools your marketing team already relies on, including email platforms, CRMs, analytics tools, ad platforms, and commerce systems. Native integrations save time; having to build custom connectors every time adds friction.
- **Campaign support:** Consider how well the CMS supports campaign execution, including ABM landing page generation, A/B testing, audience personalization, and ad-specific content variants.

### General/other considerations

- **Budget and pricing model**: Compare subscription costs, enterprise pricing, and add-on or premium feature costs.
- **Support and documentation**: Ensure the CMS provides strong documentation and direct assistance that meets your pricing tier, if needed.
- **Integrations**: Check whether the CMS integrates with the tools your team uses, and whether there's a marketplace for apps and plugins.

## FAQs

### What is a headless CMS?

A headless CMS is a content repository that stores and manages your content separately from how it appears on your website or app. You create and structure content in the backend, then deliver it through APIs to any frontend you build.

Because it doesn’t control presentation, you’re free to use any framework or technology to set up the presentation layer. That separation is what makes it “headless.”

### What is the difference between headless and traditional CMS?

The main difference is how content and presentation are handled.

A traditional CMS manages both in one system. You create content, and it’s displayed using built-in themes or templates.

A headless CMS separates those layers. You store content in the backend, fetch it using REST/GraphQL APIs, and deliver to your frontend. This approach offers greater flexibility and makes scaling easier.

### Which headless CMS is best for ecommerce?

Ecommerce can be complex because you’re managing product content, promotions, campaigns, and multiple channels all at once. You need a CMS that can deliver content quickly, stay flexible for different storefronts, and integrate seamlessly with your commerce systems.

Here are the features ecommerce teams should pay attention to.

• You need a CMS that lets you structure product and campaign content so it can be reused across multiple pages and channels.

• The CMS should deliver content through fast, reliable APIs so that pages load quickly and provide a smooth shopping experience.

• It should integrate easily with commerce platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, or custom backends to keep product and pricing data in sync.

• Templates and reusable components are important because they let you launch new product pages or campaigns without starting from scratch each time.

• Personalization features help you show different content to different customer segments to increase engagement and conversions.

• Support for localization and multiple markets is essential if you’re selling in different regions with different languages or regulatory requirements.

• SEO tools built into the CMS help your product and landing pages rank better in search results.

Workflow and approval systems help your team manage seasonal launches and campaigns efficiently, without confusion or delays.

Several platforms on this list handle ecommerce well, depending on your setup. Developers can set up reusable slices for product pages, promotions, and landing pages, while marketing teams can quickly assemble, preview, and publish content without raising a ticket.

### Which headless CMS is best for enterprises?

For enterprise needs, you want a platform that offers robust security, reliability, scalability, and strong governance while supporting multiple teams and markets. You can consider platforms like Contentful, Sanity, Prismic, or Strapi, which provide flexible content modeling, reliable API performance, and tools to manage complex workflows.

The right platform depends on the size of your content operation and the number of teams that need access.

### Is a headless CMS better for SEO?

Yes. Using a headless CMS can improve your SEO rankings in several ways:

• **Faster loading times:** Because the frontend is separate from the backend, your site doesn’t have to process heavy CMS code on every request. Faster pages improve user experience and are a positive signal for search engines.

• **Use of SEO-friendly technologies:** You can build your frontend with SEO-friendly frameworks like Next.js or Nuxt.js.

• **Full control over technical SEO:** You can manage structured data, metadata, sitemaps, canonical URLs, and other on-page SEO elements exactly how you want.

• **Better scalability for content:** As your site grows, you can optimize page structure, lazy-load resources, and manage large content sets without slowing down performance.

• **Access to your code for optimization:** Since you control the frontend, you can tweak your code for even greater performance, fine-tuning load speed and user experience to boost SEO.

### Are headless CMS platforms more expensive?

Not necessarily. Cost ultimately depends on several factors:

• The number of users

• The plan or tier you choose

• The amount of storage and API usage

• The resources and integrations your project requires

• The platform itself, as some are more expensive than others

Some providers, like Prismic, offer free forever plans that are ideal for small projects or testing, making it easier to get started without a large upfront investment.

### Can small businesses use headless CMS?

Yes, headless CMSs are ideal for small businesses. However, it depends on the amount of resources you have.

One important thing to consider is development. A headless CMS requires a developer to build and maintain the frontend that connects to the CMS via APIs. That’s an upfront cost you wouldn’t deal with if you were using a traditional CMS.

Even so, that initial investment can pay off over time. As your business grows, your team expands, or you add new digital channels, a headless CMS is much easier to scale. You won’t need to rebuild your entire content system every time you redesign your site or adopt a new technology, which makes it a strong choice for long-term growth and future-proofing.

It’s also worth noting that some headless CMS platforms, like Prismic, offer free plans. That makes it easier to get started without large platform costs. This makes it easier for small businesses to manage expenses while still building on a flexible foundation.
